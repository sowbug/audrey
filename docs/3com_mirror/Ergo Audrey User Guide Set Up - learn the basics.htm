Set Up - learn the basics

tapping

Hold Audrey's stylus as you would a pen. Lightly tap the screen element you wish to select with the point of the stylus. You do not need to tap the screen very hard. Tapping or pressing down too hard can damage the screen or degrade screen image quality.

scribbling

Some text windows allow you to scribble on the screen using the stylus, just as you would with a pen on paper. Take care not to press down too hard when writing to avoid damaging the screen.

scroll controls


Scroll controls allow you to scroll up or down through a list of items too long to fit on a screen or in a window at one time. Tap the top part of the control to scroll items up or tap the bottom part of the control to scroll the items down.

drop-down menus


Drop-down menus have a down arrow to the right of the menu display. Tap directly into the text box or on the down arrow to show the options available within the menu. The item displayed in the text window when the menu is collapsed is the selected item.

vertical selection menus


Vertical selection menus allow you to scroll through a range of values included in the text box. Tap the top arrow to scroll up or tap the bottom arrow to scroll down.

horizontal selection menus


Horizontal selection arrows allow you to increase or decrease the value contained in the text box. Tap the left arrow to decrease the value or tap the right arrow to increase the value.

scroll bars


You will see scroll bars to the right and bottom of certain windows that allow you to scroll to view information that will not fit completely inside a window. These scroll bars can be moved left and right or up and down. To move a scroll bar, either tap the scroll bar and then drag the stylus in the direction you wish to scroll or tap the arrows at the top and bottom or left and right of the scroll bar to scroll in the direction indicated by the arrow.

Typing


Use the keyboard to enter information into text boxes and text windows. The keyboard is wireless. The keyboard should be used within reading range of the screen to operate properly. There should be no objects between the keyboard and Audrey.
